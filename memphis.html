Memphis

We hope you’ll explore and eat your way through Memphis while you’re in town. Here are some of our favorite activities:

Duck March at the Peabody Hotel

Address: 149 Union Ave
Cost: Free! And you should treat yourself to a cocktail while you watch
Hours: Daily, 11:00am & 5:00pm


In 1940 Bellman Pembroke, the original duckmaster, began bringing ducks to the Peabody lobby fountain each day and taught them the now-famous Peabody Duck March. Today, the famous ducks, when off-duty, live in their Royal Duck Palace on the hotel rooftop which you can visit! Duck is not served anywhere at The Peabody. Good news!

Civil Rights Museum

Address: 450 Mulberry St
Cost: $15 adults
Hours: 9 - 6


The National Civil Rights Museum honors the site where Dr. Martin Luther King was assassinated, the infamous Lorraine Motel. Exhibits trace the history of the Civil Rights Movement in the United States from the 17th century to the present. This is an incredible museum and definitely worth a visit if you can only make it to one museum during your stay.

The Stax Museum of American Soul Music

Address: 926 East McLemore Avenue
Cost: $13 adults
Hours: 10-5 (Mon-Sat), 1-5 (Sun)

The Stax Museum is the world’s only museum dedicated to preserving and promoting the legacy of American soul music, which is the best music. Located on the original site of the Stax Records studio, the museum pays special tribute to the artists who recorded there, including: Otis Redding, Isaac Hayes, Booker T & the MGs, Sam & Dave, and Mavis Staples.

Graceland: The Home of Elvis Presley

Address: 3765 Elvis Presley Blvd.
Cost: $38.75 for basic tour. Other (more expensive) tours available
Hours: 9 – 5 (Mon-Sat), 9 – 4 (Sun)

The home and estate of Elvis Presley, our King. When you enter Graceland, you’ll follow in the same steps as Elvis himself on an iPad tour narrated by John Stamos, featuring commentary and stories by Elvis and his daughter Lisa Marie. See where Elvis lived, relaxed and spent time with his friends and family. It’s fairly tacky by today’s standards, but still pretty cool to see.

Sun Studios

Address: 706 Union Avenue
Cost: $12
Hours: 10 – 6

The true birthplace of Rock & Roll, where legends like Elvis, Johnny Cash, and Jerry Lee Lewis recorded albums that made their mark on the history of music. Sun Studio is one of the most famous and important recording studios in the world for a very good reason—this is the place where the blending of blues and country tunes gave birth to rock 'n' roll. A very good place to buy a T-shirt.

Memphis Rock & Soul Museum

Address: 191 Beale St
Cost: $12
Hours: 10 – 7

A comprehensive Memphis music experience. Exhibitions about the birth of rock and soul music, created by the Smithsonian Institution, tell the story of musical pioneers who, for the love of music, overcame racial and socio-economic barriers to create the music that shook the entire world.

Beale Street

Cost: Free, but no guns allowed
Hours: All the time, best at night

Beale Street runs from the Mississippi River to East Street for about 1.8 miles. Walk around, drink a Big Ass Beer, listen to some live music, watch the Beale Street Flippers, and enjoy being in the South. We’ll be going out here after the wedding, so stay tuned!



Bass Pro Shop

Address: 1 Bass Pro Dr
Cost: Free, but you may end up leaving with a camo bathing suit
Hours: 8 - 10

Housed in the massive iconic Pyramid where the Memphis Grizzlies used to play, this is a monstrosity. This Bass Pro Shop store includes a wilderness hotel called Big Cypress Lodge, 600,000 gallons of water features, a cypress swamp, live alligators, and an observation deck with the best view of Memphis – accessed by North America’s tallest free-standing elevator. It costs $10 to ride to the top, but there’s a good place to grab a drink once you’re there. None of this is a joke.
